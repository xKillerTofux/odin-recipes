Butternut Squash Soup

Description

Butternut Squash Soup makes an excellent meal on a cold or rainy day, as well as a great starter dish for a larger multi-course meal. The natural sweetness derived from the butternut squash pairs nicely with a multitude of different breads and salads, making it an excellent choice. It's also quite simple to make!

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 jalepeño chile pepper, seeded and diced
  • 2 teaspoons grated fresh ginger
  • 1 (2 lb) butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can chicken broth
  • 1 (12 fluid oz) can evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme, or to taste
  1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, jalepeño, and ginger; cook and stir until onion has sotened and turned translucent, about 7 minutes.
  2. Add butternut squash and chicken broth; bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until squash is tender, about 30 minutes
  3. Stir in evaporated milk, coconut milk, sugar, salt, and black pepper; cook and stir 5 minutes more.
  4. Fill a blender halfway with soup. Cover and hold lid down with a folded kitchen towel; pulse a few times before leaving on to purée. Pour into a clean pot. Repeat with remaining soupp. Or purée soup with an immersion blender until smooth. Garnish servings with sour cream and thyme.
